About the Film

Gudumba Shankar is a 2004 Telugu action comedy directed by the dynamic Puri Jagannadh. The film starred Pawan Kalyan as Shankar, a mass entertainer role that showcased his full range — from hard-hitting action to laugh-out-loud comedy.

This Puri Jagannadh–Pawan Kalyan collaboration was a commercial entertainer that clicked with mass audiences. The film features Meka Suri, one of the most iconic antagonist characters in Telugu cinema, played by Prakash Raj.

Pawan Kalyan's star power was at full display — his fight scenes, one-liners, and mass appeal made Gudumba Shankar a thoroughly enjoyable theatrical experience.

Story

Shankar is a happy-go-lucky young man who gets entangled in a conflict with the powerful villain Meka Suri. What follows is a masala action entertainer filled with comedy, emotion, and high-octane action sequences. The story is a classic good-vs-evil narrative driven by Pawan Kalyan's charismatic performance.

Why It's Special

  • First collaboration between Pawan Kalyan and director Puri Jagannadh
  • Prakash Raj's "Meka Suri" became one of Telugu cinema's most quoted villain roles
  • Pawan Kalyan's mass screen presence was at its electric best
  • Commercially successful — ran for over 100 days
  • Dialogues from the film became fan favorites and are still quoted today
