Kushi

Love, laughter, and the magic of togetherness

Director: S.J. Suryah Music: Mani Sharma Genre: Romantic Comedy Language: Telugu

About the Film

Kushi is a 2002 Telugu romantic comedy directed by S.J. Suryah, who also played the lead in the original Tamil version. The Telugu remake starred Pawan Kalyan and Bhumika Chawla in the lead roles, and became one of the most beloved films of the early 2000s Telugu cinema.

The film follows Siddhu (Pawan Kalyan) and Madhu (Bhumika Chawla), two people who constantly bicker and argue, yet find themselves drawn to each other. Their love-hate relationship forms the heart of the story, packed with comedy, romance, and emotions.

Pawan Kalyan's effortless comedy timing, energetic screen presence, and the electric chemistry with Bhumika Chawla made Kushi a timeless classic. The film was a massive commercial blockbuster and ran for over 175 days in theaters.

Story

Siddhu is a carefree, fun-loving young man who meets Madhu during a train journey. Their personalities clash instantly — but fate keeps bringing them together. What begins as constant quarreling slowly transforms into a deep, genuine love. The film beautifully captures the chaos and joy of falling in love with someone who is your complete opposite.

Why It's Special

  • Bhumika Chawla won the Filmfare Award for Best Actress (Telugu)
  • Ran for 175+ days — a massive box office success
  • Considered a benchmark romantic comedy in Telugu cinema
